I am a PhD student in the Department of Psychology at the University of Minnesota. I study auditory perception in Dr. Andrew Oxenham's Auditory Perception and Cognition Lab.

Formerly, I earned a BS in Psychology from the School of Behavioral and Brain Sciences at the University of Texas at Dallas. I worked in Dr. Peter Assmann's Speech Perception Lab, where I focused on perception of indexical properties in children's speech by normal-hearing and cochlear-implant listeners.

I have a broad basis of training in psychology, speech and hearing, and mathematics. Additionally, I have experience with programming in a number of languages, statistical modeling, digital signal processing, and creating publications with LaTeX.
